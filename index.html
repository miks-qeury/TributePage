Ivan Pavlov

Russian and Soviet scientist, the first Russian Nobel laureate

Soviet ideologization

After his death, Pavlov was transformed into a symbol of Soviet science, his scientific exploit was also considered as an ideological feat (in some sense the "Pavlov school" (or Pavlov's teaching) became an ideological phenomenon). Under the slogan "Protection of the Pavlovian Heritage", the so-called "Pavlov Session" of the Academy of Sciences of the USSR and the USSR Academy of Medical Sciences (organizers - KM Bykov, A. G. Ivanov-Smolensky) was held in 1950, where the leading physiologists of the country were persecuted. Such a policy, however, was in sharp contradiction with Pavlov's own views

Timeline

  • 1849 - Ivan Pavlov Is Born
  • 1870 - Pavlov enrolls in a course of natural sciences at the University of St. Petersburg, abandoning his religious career.
  • 1879 - Pavlov graduates from the Military-Medical Academy of the University of St. Petersburg and finishes his studies at the Academy of Medical Surgery, earning his first gold medal.
  • 1884 - Pavlov gives a lecutre on physiology at his alma mater in the Military-Medical Academy.
  • 1890 - Pavlov is appointed a chair position at the Military-Medical Academy in the department of pharmacology.
  • 1891 - Pavlov becomes the director of physiology at the University of St. Petersburg in the Institute of Experimental Medicine.
  • 1897 - Pavlov publishes his lecutres on the nervous system, entitled "Lectures on the functions of the principal digestive glands."
  • 1904 - Pavlov is awarded his Nobel Prize for his work and discoveries in the field of physiology and digestive glands.
  • 1921 - Ivan Pavlov receives a government decree, signed by Lenin, for his work in physiology.
  • 1936 - Pavlov Dies
